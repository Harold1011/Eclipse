    Setting a GHG reduction target makes business sense. There are many studies, including some by WWF and CDP that state that reducing carbon emissions results in annual savings, increasing productivity and it also fosters innovation in organizations.


    We encourage our suppliers to set a target that is in line with the 1.5C decarbonization approach, but most importantly, it should be an ambitious but realistic goal, based on your company’s needs and maturity. Your goal can be an absolute goal or an intensity goal.

    Examples of goals are:

    • • Absolute: Reduce GHG emission 10% year over year
    • • Absolute: Reduce GHG emissions by 20% by 2025
    • • Intensity: Reduce GHG emissions per tonnage (or revenue) by 50% by 2030

    We encourage our suppliers to set a target that takes direct action and impacts watershed protection around areas where they operate.

    Examples of goals are:

    • • Reduce water usage by 20%
    • • 100% of high water risks sites will be identified and directly impacted

    We encourage our suppliers to impact positively in recycling initiatives that result in an increase in recycled content on their products. The ideal target should be measurable and impact the world of circular economy.

    Examples of goals are:

    • • 100% of packaging will be made out of majority recycled content
    • • 50% of products will be made out of alternative materials
